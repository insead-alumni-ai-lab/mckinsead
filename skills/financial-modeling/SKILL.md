# Financial Modeling

## When to use
When building or validating financial models for strategy engagements: revenue builds, cost structures, DCF valuations, scenario models.

## Key Principles
1. **Revenue build**: Top-down (TAM × share) AND bottom-up (units × price). Cross-check both.
2. **Cost structure**: Fixed vs. variable. Always model as % of revenue AND absolute numbers.
3. **Sensitivity analysis**: Identify 3-5 key drivers. Show tornado chart of impact.
4. **Scenario modeling**: Base, upside, downside. Define assumptions for each clearly.
5. **Sanity checks**: Does the margin structure match industry benchmarks? Does growth rate imply realistic customer acquisition?

## Common Pitfalls
- Hockey stick projections without justification
- Forgetting working capital in cash flow models
- Mixing real and nominal rates
- Not stress-testing key assumptions
- Circular references in Excel-style models

## Output Format
- Always show assumptions table separately from calculations
- Include unit economics where applicable
- Bridge charts for year-over-year changes
- Waterfall charts for margin analysis
