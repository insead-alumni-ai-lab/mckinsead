# SCQA Framing

## When to use
Stage 1 (Scoping) — frame the strategic problem.

## SCQA Components
- **S**ituation: Stable context the audience agrees with
- **C**omplication: What changed or went wrong (creates tension)
- **Q**uestion: The natural question arising from the complication
- **A**nswer: Your hypothesis or recommendation

## Example
- S: "ACME Corp has been the market leader in widgets for 15 years with 35% share."
- C: "Over the past 18 months, two new entrants with AI-powered widgets have captured 12% of the market."
- Q: "Should ACME invest in AI capabilities, acquire a competitor, or focus on its existing moat?"
- A: "ACME should pursue a dual strategy: fast-follow AI development while acquiring SmartWidget Co."

## Best Practices
- Keep the Situation to 1-2 sentences (don't overload context)
- The Complication should create urgency
- The Question must be answerable
- The Answer must be falsifiable
