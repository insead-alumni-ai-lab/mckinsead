# Quick Math / Back-of-Envelope

## When to use
For rapid estimation, sanity checks, and sizing exercises during strategy engagements.

## Techniques
1. **Market sizing**: Population × penetration × frequency × price
2. **Rule of 72**: Years to double = 72 / growth rate %
3. **80/20 checks**: Does 20% of X drive 80% of Y? Quick Pareto validation.
4. **Order of magnitude**: Is this billions, millions, or thousands? Get the right order first.
5. **Comparison anchors**: Compare to known quantities (e.g., "that's roughly the GDP of Belgium")

## Application in Frameworks
- **BCG Matrix**: Quick relative market share calc (your share / #1 competitor share)
- **Porter 5**: HHI approximation (sum of squared market shares × 10000)
- **Value Chain**: Cost share % from P&L line items
- **Hypothesis testing**: Quick feasibility check before full analysis

## Rules
- Always state assumptions explicitly
- Round aggressively — precision is false confidence at this stage
- If the quick math says the hypothesis is off by 10x, it's probably wrong
- Use Fermi estimation when hard data is unavailable
